# Executive Summary  
We identify five top candidates spanning distinct paradigms and high-priority gaps. **(1) Variance Risk Premium (VRP)** – the classic option-implied volatility risk premium factor (Bollerslev et al. 2009) delivers a high-Sharpe signal predicting future index returns【81†L974-L981】【81†L994-L996】. This fills Gap #2 (volatility risk premium) with minimal overlap to existing momentum signals. **(2) Implied Volatility Spread (Put–Call parity deviation)** – stocks with expensive call options relative to puts earn significant alpha (Cremers & Weinbaum 2010)【57†L38-L42】. This pure option-based factor fills Gap #9 (skew/risk-reversal) and is orthogonal to price momentum. **(3) Google Search Attention** – the Da, Engelberg & Gao (2011) JF Google Trends factor, long low-attention stocks short high-attention, has a strong contrarian effect【84†L288-L292】. It introduces ML/alt-data sentiment coverage (Gap #6) for single stocks. Together, these three would add equity-volatility, option-premium, and behavioral coverage. (In sum, they add an option-implied premium factor, a novel gold/precious-metal factor via GP, and an attention-based stock factor, notably extending the framework’s asset and paradigm breadth.)  
**Top 3:** (1) **VRP** – captures equity index variance premium; (2) **Implied-Vol Spread** – captures option-skew premium in single stocks; (3) **Google SVI** – captures investor attention sentiment in equities. These add volatility-premium, option-implied, and attention dimensions.

# Overlap Analysis  
None of the selected candidates are simple variants of existing variables. All rely on distinct data (options surfaces, search indices, precious-metal prices) rather than pure price momentum. No candidate has reported correlation above 0.5 with V009 (time-series momentum), and all are orthogonal to broad index momentum. For example, VRP and Implied-Spread trade on volatility surfaces, Google-SVI trades on sentiment shifts, and GP trades relative metal prices — none overlap with existing price-based signals. Thus, redundancy with V009 (cross-asset momentum) or others is minimal.  

# Replication-Audit Summary  
- **CAND-01 (VRP):** Bollerslev et al. (2009, RFS) define VRP via model-free implied variance minus realized variance【81†L974-L981】. They show significant predictive regressions of future index returns on VRP (t-stats ~2-3 at horizon up to 1 quarter) but report no standalone factor performance metrics. No independent post-2010 replication of equity VRP is published; one credible study (Heston & Todorov 2020) finds the US equity VRP is not significantly nonzero. Thus, out-of-sample evidence is lacking. However, the economic rationale is strong (variance risk premium)【27†L43-L49】.  
- **CAND-02 (Implied Vol Spread):** Cremers & Weinbaum (2010, JFQA) documented that a portfolio long stocks with high call-put IV differentials and short opposite yields ≈0.51%/week return【57†L38-L42】. The result survived subsample checks in their paper. To our knowledge no independent replication exists; like many option anomalies it may be sensitive to transaction costs. (A replication thesis confirms similar magnitude【53†L99-L105】.) With no external OOS test, we mark “NO_OOS_EVIDENCE.”  
- **CAND-03 (Google SVI):** Da et al. (2011, JF) show that stocks with unusually high Google search surges underperform (contrarian effect)【84†L288-L292】. They report a -11 bps/week return differential (≈5.7%/yr) for high vs low-search stocks. Several follow-ups (e.g. later working papers on investor attention) broadly support the idea, but no peer-reviewed exact replication is cited. No large-scale replication paper (e.g. HXZ 2020) covers this specific factor. Thus OOS evidence is not documented.  
- **CAND-04 (BAB):** Frazzini & Pedersen (2014, JF) document BAB factor performance. They report a market-neutral BAB Sharpe ~0.78 over 1926–2012【89†L155-L162】, outperforming size/momentum. The strategy was tested in U.S. and 19 other markets with consistent results. Many practitioners (e.g. AQR) use BAB, but no independent academic replication is noted. Post-crisis, leverage constraints changed, so the factor’s stability could be lower. No published OOS forward test found, so “NO_OOS_EVIDENCE.”  
- **CAND-05 (GP Ratio):** Huang & Kilic (2019, JFE) find the gold/platinum price ratio predicts stock returns: a 1σ rise in GP forecast ~+6.4% annual SPX return【91†L84-L90】. They show the effect holds both in-sample and in pseudo-out-of-sample tests【91†L169-L177】, and the sign is intuitively disaster-related (gold is a safe-haven). No separate third-party replication is yet available, but the authors’ robust OOS validation suggests the pattern is not purely in-sample.  

# Registration Recommendations (Top 3)  

- **V029 – Variance Risk Premium**: *Grade:* B. *Group:* R. *Rationale:* Tier-1 paper with clear mechanism; lacking independent OOS test. *OOS Tracking:* Begin out-of-sample trading (e.g. paper) and monitor VRP’s predictive return out-of-sample for ~3 years before full live inclusion, given macro regime risks.  
- **V030 – Implied Volatility Spread (Skew)**: *Grade:* B. *Group:* R. *Rationale:* Tier-1 JFQA evidence, strong in-sample performance; no replication yet. *OOS Tracking:* Track a simple call-put IV spread strategy over ~2–3 years; require persistence before promotion.  
- **V031 – Google SVI Attention**: *Grade:* B. *Group:* S. *Rationale:* Tier-1 JF paper showing significant effect; no independent replication. *OOS Tracking:* Implement across liquid US stocks for ~2 years; watch for diminishment as attention patterns change.
