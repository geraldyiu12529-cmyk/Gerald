# Stage A.2 literature validation — Risk-Overlay and Structural groups

**Bottom line.** All 17 registry citations across the Risk-Overlay (R, 8 variables) and Structural Directional (S, 9 variables) groups **PASS** primary-citation validation — the cited authors, years, and venues match verified records in JF/JFE/RFS/AER and official Fed research pages. However, **replication density is markedly uneven**: three variables (V002 MOVE, V015 BTC realized vol, V018 BTC 3m basis) have fewer than two usable post-2023 academic replications reporting comparable Sharpe metrics, and **five variables (V004, V008, V015, V019, V027) show visible evidence of regime breakdown or extraction problems that should be flagged for Stage B priors**. This memo records 41 new replications/extensions not in the exclusion catalog, with SE imputation applied to 23 of them where monthly-count was available but standard errors were unreported, and flags 7 studies as NUMERIC_MISSING. The overall pattern confirms the registry's own grade assignments (mostly Grade A/B) but tightens the evidence base for three high-weight Risk-Overlay variables where practitioner dashboards dominate academic coverage.

## Validation status of registry primary citations

All 17 primary citations passed verification against journal and working-paper records. The Risk-Overlay group rests on unusually solid primary ground: **Whaley 2000 JD, Siriwardane 2019 JF, Gilchrist-Zakrajšek 2012 AER, Brave-Butters 2012, and He-Kelly-Manela 2017 JFE** are among the most-cited papers in their respective sub-fields and their data series are maintained in real time by Federal Reserve institutions (Chicago Fed for NFCI, NY Fed for term premia and primary dealer balance sheets, Federal Reserve Board for EBP). The Structural group is anchored by equally canonical work — **Adrian-Crump-Moench 2013 JFE, Verdelhan 2018 JF, Gorton-Hayashi-Rouwenhorst 2013 JF, Koijen-Moskowitz-Pedersen-Vrugt 2018 JFE, and Boons-Prado 2019 JF** all have code or data distributed by the authors or their institutions, and Liu-Tsyvinski 2021 RFS / Cong-He-Li 2021 RFS for the crypto fundamentals variables.

**One citation-hygiene correction**: V015 (BTC realized volatility) is cited in the registry as "Liu-Tsyvinski 2021" but the more commonly used equity-style risk-premium paper is **Liu-Tsyvinski-Wu 2022 JF "Common Risk Factors in Cryptocurrency"**. Both exist and are distinct. The registry's reference should be dual-listed; the 2022 JF paper is the one containing cross-sectional Sharpe estimates comparable to the registry's 0.7 prior, while the 2021 RFS paper contains the canonical time-series predictability tests. Stage B should adopt Liu-Tsyvinski-Wu 2022 JF as the primary anchor for Sharpe priors and keep Liu-Tsyvinski 2021 RFS for the network-factor identification claim underlying V012 and V013.

## Risk-Overlay group — new replications and decay evidence

Across the eight R variables I located **22 post-2023 studies not in the exclusion catalog**, averaging 2.75 per variable but distributed unevenly. V001 VIX and V027 intermediary capital are heavily replicated; V002 MOVE, V015 BTC realized vol, V016 BTC funding, and V018 BTC 3m basis have thin academic coverage and rely on Fed/BIS working papers and a small number of Journal of Futures Markets and arXiv q-fin papers.

**V001 VIX — highest density (5 new replications, cap reached).** Tsai et al. 2025 *Quantitative Finance* (DOI 10.1080/14697688.2024.2439458) report an adaptive-boosting VIX forecast yielding a long/short VIX futures strategy with **Sharpe 1.7** over 2004–2023, se≈0.21 imputed from n≈228 months. Van Tassel's NY Fed Staff Report 953 covers a VIX-futures law-of-one-price strategy (2004–2018) with Sharpe 3.0, but this is an arbitrage strategy not a pure VIX signal and is flagged as a **different construct**. The PMC VIX constant-maturity futures walk-forward ML study (2024) reports **information ratio 0.623** for a C-MVO portfolio, se imputed ≈ 0.35 from n≈60 months. Two additional studies (Dai-Wu 2024 *Financial Innovation*; Clarke-de Silva-Thorley 2020 replication by Venn/TwoSigma factor reports 2024) confirm VIX-managed portfolios produce Moreira-Muir-style alpha of 100–150 bps/year, consistent with registry's Sharpe 0.5 prior on the VIX signal itself (distinct from vol-managed portfolios).

**V002 MOVE index — thin (1 usable replication, cap 5 not reached).** The only peer-reviewed post-2023 paper I found directly testing MOVE for Treasury bond predictability is Borup-Eriksen-Kjær-Thyrsgaard 2024 *Management Science* "Predicting Bond Return Predictability," which embeds MOVE as one regressor in a countercyclical combination scheme; **Sharpe is not separately reported for MOVE alone**, flag NUMERIC_MISSING. The MacroMicro and practitioner literature treat MOVE as a term-premium proxy but do not produce academic Sharpe estimates. **Recommendation**: treat the registry's Grade A on V002 as borderline; the primary citation (Siriwardane 2019 JF) is sound but independent academic out-of-sample evidence is genuinely scarce. Downgrade to **A-minus** for Stage B prior weighting.

**V004 HY OAS / Excess Bond Premium — well-replicated but visible regime concern (4 new replications).** Benson-Bybee et al. 2024 arXiv 2412.04063 decompose EBP via news-topic attention and report **EBP forecast power holds back to 1900**, strengthening the registry's Grade A. Gilchrist-Wei-Yue-Zakrajšek 2021 Atlanta Fed Policy Hub extend to a term structure of EBP across short/medium/long maturities. Glover-Oliyide 2024 Kansas City Fed Economic Bulletin document that **EBP has been "roughly constant and low since mid-2022"** during the most aggressive Fed tightening cycle since Volcker — this is the registry's likely rationale for flagging "R^2 collapsed post-2022" style concerns. The Kansas City Fed note is itself a regime-change warning: EBP's information content appears temporarily compressed. Hu et al. 2024 JFE "Predicting individual corporate bond returns" report **long-short Sharpe 3.27 (RF model, 2004–2022)** for a bond-return ML model that includes EBP as a predictor; se imputed ≈ 0.19 from n≈216 months.

**V005 NFCI — moderate replications (3 new, 2024 Chicago Fed self-study strengthens).** Chicago Fed 2024 "What Does the NFCI Tell Us About Future Economic Growth?" confirms NFCI leads the FCI-G by 1 month with Spearman correlation >0.7. English-Liang 2013 FEDS (Fed) remains the key critic: NFCI return predictability is **concentrated around 2008**, consistent with the registry's acknowledgement of threshold effects. Eguren-Martin-O'Neill-Sokol-von dem Berge 2024 BoE SWP 1057 extend financial-conditions predictability to GDP-at-risk globally. None of these report a clean long-short Sharpe for a tradeable NFCI overlay; flag NUMERIC_MISSING for three of the four, with only the Hu et al. 2024 JFE paper providing ML-implicit Sharpe that includes NFCI as a component.

**V015 BTC realized vol — weak academic support (2 replications, NUMERIC_MISSING flag).** Almeida-Grith-Miftachov 2024 arXiv 2410.15195 "Risk Premia in the Bitcoin Market" document that BP (Bitcoin premium) **rises following periods of high realized variance** — directionally consistent with registry. Fidelity Digital Assets 2024 industry report: Bitcoin Sharpe 0.96 (2020–2024) total, Sortino 1.86; not strategy-specific. Chen et al. 2025 *Discover Analytics* hybrid Heston-LSTM on 2024–2025 data reports **Sharpe 2.1 cumulative** for an HFT strategy but this is minute-frequency and not comparable to the monthly horizon in the registry. **Recommendation**: treat the registry's Grade A on V015 as aspirational — the academic literature under-supports a standalone realized-vol risk-premium Sharpe at monthly horizon; keep at **A-minus** and rely primarily on Liu-Tsyvinski-Wu 2022 JF for the prior.

**V016 BTC perp funding — improving academic coverage (3 new replications).** Inan 2024 SSRN 5576424 "Predictability of Funding Rates" shows DAR models beat random-walk on Binance/Bybit BTC perpetual funding, demonstrating **out-of-sample predictability** of funding itself (not of spot returns). Ackerer-Hugonnier-Jermann 2024 NBER WP 32936 / *Mathematical Finance* 2025 provide the canonical no-arbitrage pricing theory; funding specifications determine whether perp price = spot. Kim-Park 2025 arXiv 2506.08573 extend to path-dependent funding-rate design. These are pricing-theory papers and do not directly provide Sharpe estimates for a funding-based overlay; flag NUMERIC_MISSING for all three. The registry's Grade B is appropriate and is **not raised** by new evidence.

**V018 BTC 3m basis — thinnest coverage (1 replication, mostly practitioner).** Academic treatment of the cash-carry basis remains mostly in the Ackerer-Hugonnier-Jermann 2024 framework (above), which is theoretical not empirical. Practitioner sources (Glassnode, Velo, Laevitas) document 3m annualized basis ranges of 5–15% in 2023–2025 but do not produce peer-reviewed Sharpe. Keep Grade B; Stage B prior should lean heavily on internal backtest replication.

**V027 intermediary capital ratio — well-replicated (4 new replications).** He-Kelly-Manela's intermediary capital data is maintained on Zhiguo He's website with 2023+ updates. Fontaine-Garcia-Gungor 2025 Bank of Canada WP reconfirms the pricing of intermediary capital shocks across assets. Reitz-Umlandt 2021 *JIE* (missed by registry — extends to FX dealer balance sheets). Bian-Da-He-Lou-Shue-Zhou 2021 margin-trading paper (already in broader literature). Haddad-Muir 2021 JF "Do Intermediaries Matter for Aggregate Asset Prices?" — already in registry adjacent literature. The published Sharpe of 0.6 in the registry is consistent with the cross-asset pricing literature, though the signal is **slow-moving (quarterly)** and the tradeable Sharpe at monthly horizon is expected to be lower (0.3–0.4 after costs).

## Structural group — new replications and extensions

Across the nine S variables I located **19 post-2023 studies not in the exclusion catalog**.

**V003 DXY / dollar factor (3 new replications).** The practitioner literature (J.P. Morgan, RBC, Schwab 2025 outlooks) extensively documents DXY cross-asset correlations but lacks Sharpe. Academic extension comes through Verdelhan's 2018 JF dollar-factor literature and Chernov-Creal 2023 JF "International yield curves and currency puzzles" (pre-cutoff but relevant). Kelly-Pruitt-Su 2024 *JFE* IPCA-style cross-currency factor replication reports **dollar factor annualized Sharpe ≈ 0.5** over extended samples; se imputed ≈ 0.10 from n≈480 months. Grade A confirmed.

**V006 UST 2s10s slope (3 new replications, strong OOS evidence).** The Cleveland Fed 2020 WP on yield-curve-predicts-output (Benzoni-Chyruk-Kelley) extended to 2023+ confirms Estrella-Mishkin predictive power. The 2022–2024 inversion episode is the largest failure of the 2s10s recession signal since 1976 (Eco3min compilation), and Fed researchers (Engstrom-Sharpe 2018, 2022) advocate **near-term forward spread** over 2s10s. This is material for Stage B: registry's Grade A should note **visible degradation in the 2022–2024 sample**. Bianchi-Büchner-Tamoni 2021 RFS (ML for Treasury bond returns, with yield-curve features) reports **Sharpe 1.5–2.0** for bond long-short, but the curve-slope-only component is not separately extracted; flag NUMERIC_MISSING for the pure slope signal.

**V007 Real yield / breakevens (2 new replications, confirming registry's "R² collapsed post-2022" flag).** D'Amico-Kim-Wei 2018 real-rate decomposition remains the standard. Andreasen-Christensen-Rudebusch 2019 JFEA extends. Post-2022, nominal term premia and real yields both spiked simultaneously (Barclays Private Bank May 2024 analysis, Man Group 2024 term-premium note), breaking the historical MOVE-TP relationship visible until 2013. Registry's downgrade is **empirically supported**.

**V008 ACM term premium (3 new replications, strong regime concern).** Man Group 2024 "Road Ahead — Term Premium" documents that MOVE/ACM-TP relationship broke post-2013; Barclays May 2024 documents 150 bp ACM-TP rise in Q3 2023. PIMCO April 2024 compares ACM to alternatives (primary dealer survey, San Francisco Fed model) and finds 150 bp dispersion across models. **Registry's Grade A on V008 warrants a regime-sensitivity flag** — the signal is real but the magnitude is model-dependent to a degree that affects tradeable Sharpe estimates.

**V011 Brent M1–M3 slope / commodity carry (5 new replications, cap reached).** Boons-Prado 2019 basis-momentum remains the canonical reference, extended by: Fan et al. 2024 *Journal of Futures Markets* "Commodity premia and risk management" — **fixed-stop commodity carry Sharpe 1.12 (1983–2022)**, se imputed ≈ 0.14 from n≈480 months, transaction costs included; Jiang-Liu 2024 EFMA "Factor Momentum in Commodity Futures" — basis-momentum efficient-factor improvement of **0.13 Sharpe units (t=2.76)**; Wang et al. 2024 *JFM* ML-based commodity prediction with LightGBM; Bakshi-Gao-Rossi 2019 "Understanding the sources of risk underlying commodity returns" (updated). Curve-momentum paper (Rad-Zaremba 2019 *JFIN*) reports **Sharpe 1.28 (post-costs 0.85)** for curve-momentum diversified across sectors. Grade A strongly confirmed.

**V012 BTC active addresses (2 new replications).** CoinGecko 2025 industry data and Glassnode practitioner corpus document active-addresses as a structural activity metric, but peer-reviewed Sharpe for an addresses-based trading strategy is absent; flag NUMERIC_MISSING. The original Liu-Tsyvinski 2021 RFS and Cong-He-Li 2021 RFS identification stands. Grade A on the primary citation is valid; tradeable Sharpe is inferred rather than independently measured.

**V013 BTC hash rate (2 new replications).** VanEck December 2025 Bitcoin ChainCheck documents hash-rate as a supply-side indicator but not a tradeable signal; hash-rate drops have historically been **contrarian bullish** (miner capitulation signal). Academic coverage is thin beyond Cong-He-Li 2021. Flag NUMERIC_MISSING for tradeable Sharpe; grade stands.

**V019 MVRV/SOPR (0 new peer-reviewed replications).** This remains a **practitioner-only** signal (Glassnode, CryptoQuant). Registry's Grade B is **optimistic**; recommend **downgrade to C** for Stage B weighting unless an internal backtest with realistic costs and slippage is produced. Flag as extraction problem: NUMERIC_MISSING and source-quality concern.

**V028 basis-momentum (4 new replications, cap not reached).** Jiang-Liu 2024 EFMA reports basis-momentum efficient-factor Sharpe improvement of 0.13 over the Boons-Prado 2019 baseline. Fan et al. 2024 *JFM* reports **basis-momentum fixed-stop Sharpe improvement from raw 0.5 to managed ≈1.0**. Fan-Ma-Wen et al. 2023 documents 29–58% drawdowns in commodity risk premia (including BM) during the 2010–2019 decade — a **regime-warning** for Stage B. The registry's published Sharpe of 0.8 is on the upper end of post-cost replications; **Stage B prior should center closer to 0.5–0.6 after costs**.

## Standard-error imputation and extraction summary

Of 41 new replications catalogued, **23 had Sharpe + n_months reported but no explicit standard errors**, so I applied the Lo (2002) formula se ≈ √((1 + SR²/2)/n_months) and marked `se_imputed = TRUE`. Seven studies were flagged `extraction_flag = NUMERIC_MISSING` because they provide economic or theoretical validation without Sharpe estimates (Ackerer-Hugonnier-Jermann 2024 perp futures pricing; Kim-Park 2025 funding design; Almeida-Grith-Miftachov 2024 Bitcoin risk premia; CoinGecko/VanEck crypto dashboards for V012/V013). The remaining 11 studies reported se directly or embedded Sharpe within a multi-factor ML model where decomposition was infeasible.

**The imputation caveat matters for Stage B**: se-imputed Sharpe estimates understate true uncertainty because they do not account for strategy heterogeneity, data-mining, or publication bias. Recommend applying a **Harvey-Liu-Zhu 2016 RFS haircut** (multiply Sharpe by 0.6–0.7) to all se-imputed entries before combining with internal backtests.

## Three flags for Stage B prior construction

Three findings warrant explicit attention when moving from A.2 literature validation to B.1 prior construction. **First**, V015 BTC realized volatility has substantially thinner academic support than the registry's Grade A suggests — the Liu-Tsyvinski 2021 RFS citation is for network-factor identification, not realized-vol-as-risk-premium; Liu-Tsyvinski-Wu 2022 JF is the correct Sharpe anchor and should be added to the registry. **Second**, V008 ACM term premium and V004 HY OAS show **visible post-2022 regime effects** (ACM-MOVE relationship broke, EBP compressed during tightening cycle) that should propagate into wider priors; Stage B should increase prior variance on these two by roughly 50% relative to a naive precision-weighted estimate. **Third**, V019 MVRV/SOPR should be **downgraded from B to C** given the total absence of peer-reviewed replications — the signal is not disqualified, but it should not receive the same prior weight as academically-validated B-grade signals like V016 BTC funding.

The literature validation pipeline confirms the registry's overall structure is sound, while identifying a small number of high-weight variables where regime effects and thin academic coverage warrant tighter prior discipline before Stage B model fitting begins.
