3. Strengths — What's Working Exceptionally Well

STRENGTH Evidence-Grading Discipline

The A/B/C grading system is the backbone of the entire workflow. It's not decorative — it's load-bearing. Every variable in the scorecard carries a grade, and the Grade C prohibition prevents narrative contamination. The reconciliation notes (MVRV demotion, funding-rate filter-not-predictor) show you've thought carefully about where practitioner intuition diverges from published evidence.

STRENGTH Fail-Loud Architecture

Most trading systems fail silently — a missing data point gets interpolated or ignored, and the resulting score looks clean but isn't. Your fail-loud rule does the opposite: a MISSING Grade A variable blanks the score leg rather than inferring it, and the blank propagates up to block entry. This is the single most important safety feature in the workspace, and it worked correctly on Day 1 (EWY was +3 on paper but blocked because intermediary capital was MISSING).

STRENGTH Audit Trail Completeness

The combination of dated .md recs, .html visual reports, Memory.md §8 lessons log, §10 contribution log, and the never-digest retention rule for trade recs creates a full audit trail. Six months from now, the 2026-10-14 review will be able to look back at every day's scorecard to determine whether the three audit additions contributed. This forward-thinking design is rare.

STRENGTH Pre-Entry Checklist as Hard Gate

Risk Rules §7's 6-item pre-entry checklist is properly binding — not advisory. Items like "invalidation written, concrete, date-bounded" and "catalyst asymmetry stated" force the trade thesis to be fully specified before capital is deployed. The correlation gate (§5) prevents the common failure mode of taking three correlated positions and thinking they're diversified.

4. Weaknesses & Recommendations

GAP #1 Audit-Addition Data Pipeline — No Automated Pull Path

The three variables added on 2026-04-14 (residual momentum, intermediary capital, basis-momentum) are all Grade A and fail-loud, but none has a working automated pull. Residual momentum requires downloading FF5 factor returns from Kenneth French's library and running a 12-month rolling OLS per stock — this is a compute task, not a web-search closure. Intermediary capital requires parsing NY Fed CSV data and computing a z-score. Basis-momentum requires a historical F1–F2 settlement series. Until these are automated, the entire single-stock book stays T-blocked and the commodity S divergence-cap can't fire.

RECOMMENDATION Build a Python compute skill that runs at 19:50 UTC+8 (before the brief fires at 20:00), pulls/computes these three variables, and writes the results to a staging file the brief and trade-rec skills can consume. This is the #1 priority item — it unblocks more scorecard legs than any other improvement.

GAP #2 Same-Day Rerun Churn

On Day 1, the trade-rec reached v8 and the market brief reached v4. Multiple versions per day with no new data between runs is waste — each version is an overwrite that increases Memory.md §8's length without adding decision value. The v6→v7→v8 sequence had identical conclusions ("0 |Sum| ≥ 3 signals, no trade") because no upstream data changed.

RECOMMENDATION Add a "delta check" at the top of the trade-rec skill: before re-running the full 8-step, compare the modification timestamps of the 4 upstream artifacts against the existing rec. If none have changed, append a one-line "no-change revalidation" to §8 and skip the full rewrite. This preserves the audit trail while eliminating wasted compute.

GAP #3 Memory.md §8 Growth Rate

§8 (Lessons & Corrections) is already 180+ lines after one day of operations. Each trade-rec run appends a multi-line entry. At this rate, Memory.md will exceed 500 lines within a week, making it expensive to read at session startup. The current retention policy covers dated output files but doesn't address Memory.md internal section growth.

RECOMMENDATION Add a Memory.md condensation rule to the weekly review skill: on Sunday, roll the prior week's §8 entries into a single summary paragraph (regime, signals fired, trades taken, key lessons). Keep only the current week's entries in full detail. Archive the verbose entries to a new file memory-log-YYYY-WNN.md in the archive folder.

GAP #4 Pipeline Scheduling Gaps

The brief and news tasks share the same cron minute (0 20 * * *). In practice the jitter values separate them (96s vs 63s), but this is fragile — if both fire simultaneously, they could compete for web-search rate limits. Additionally, all five daily tasks fire every day of the week including Saturday and Sunday, when US markets are closed and most data sources won't have new readings. This creates empty or stale briefs on weekends.

RECOMMENDATION Stagger the cron expressions (brief at 20:00, news at 20:10, rec at 20:25). Restrict the daily brief, news, and rec to Mon–Fri (0 20 * * 1-5). The weekly review already correctly fires Sunday-only. Consider adding a preflight-check run at 20:15 Mon–Fri, between news and rec, to catch missing upstream artifacts before the rec fires.

GAP #5 Dual Retention Logic

The Retention Policy.md defines the master 3-tier archival system, but news-events/README.md defines its own 7-day/30-day retention rules with a separate archive-summary.md mechanism. These two systems overlap and could conflict — the weekly tidy task might try to archive a news file that the README says should be condensed into a different summary file.

RECOMMENDATION Unify: have the weekly tidy task handle news files per the master Retention Policy, and remove the independent retention section from news-events/README.md. Or explicitly exempt news files from the master policy and delegate entirely to the README rules. Either way, one system should own the lifecycle.

8. Summary Verdict

This is a remarkably well-built trading workspace for Day 2 of operations. The methodology-first approach — writing the framework, evidence grading, risk rules, and fail-loud architecture before taking any trades — is the right sequencing and it's rare. Most traders build their process retroactively after losses; you've front-loaded discipline.

The 8.4/10 overall score reflects that the intellectual architecture (methodology, risk rules, evidence grading) is essentially complete and production-quality, while the operational infrastructure (data pipelines, scheduling, memory management) has a few gaps that are normal for a system this young.

The single highest-leverage improvement is P0: building a compute skill for the three audit-addition variables. Until residual momentum, intermediary capital, and basis-momentum have automated pull paths, the fail-loud rule correctly blocks most of the book — which means the methodology is working as designed, but the workspace can't actually trade. Solving P0 transforms the workspace from "well-designed but gated" to "fully operational."
