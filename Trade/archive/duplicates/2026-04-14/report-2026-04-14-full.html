3. Analysis Methodology

Analysis follows the eight-step evidence-graded methodology defined in Methodology Prompt.md. Each candidate asset is scored on four independent legs, each ∈ {−1, 0, +1}: S (structural anchor), T (tactical confirmation), C (catalyst asymmetry), and R (risk overlay). The aggregate Sum = S+T+C+R maps to action thresholds.

Evidence-grade discipline

Every variable cited carries a grade. Grade A variables have replicated evidence and coherent economic mechanism (e.g. 12-month time-series momentum, carry, credit spreads, BTC order imbalance, BTC realized vol, BTC active addresses, exchange netflows). Grade B are regime-dependent or thinner history (ETF flows, positioning, skew, news sentiment). Grade C is not used for scoring; no stock-to-flow or halving-cycle timing is permitted.

Grade mix of variables used in today's scorecard

Binding policy overlays

  • Correlation gate: before promoting |sum|≥3, check whether the candidate shares its primary regime variable with open positions or other simultaneous signals. Co-moving theses (e.g. Copper + Gold + Silver as one reflation bet) collapse to a single sector-cap expression.
  • Risk sizing: inverse-ATR volatility targeting with quarter-Kelly overlay; per-position risk 0.5–2%; portfolio heat ≤ 6–8%; ATR stops at 2–3×, not fixed %.
  • Fail-loud data rule: a Grade-A variable marked MISSING blocks the corresponding score leg; the affected Sum is qualified, never imputed.

5. Discussion

Regime interpretation

The dominant cross-asset signal is a reflation shock driven by the Hormuz-scoped supply disruption, partially offset today by CENTCOM's narrower-than-feared blockade clarification. The market is pricing less tail risk (VIX 19 with contango term structure, MOVE 78.7 off peak, HY OAS 2.94% benign) but still pricing the energy tax (Brent holding ~$98, gold at record, breakevens 2.36%). This creates a recognisable reflation-DXY-weak theme across metals that is structurally alive but tactically extended — the canonical "don't chase" configuration.

Why the correlation gate dominates today

Copper, Gold and Silver each score +2. If any promoted to +3, Risk Rules §5 would force them into a single sector-cap expression rather than three independent positions. That constraint matters because it converts what looks like breadth into concentration: adding Gold and Silver on top of Copper would not diversify the book, it would triple the reflation exposure while pretending to diversify. The cleanest structural + tactical combo without an exhausted-move flag is Copper, which is also the only candidate lacking only a catalyst — the most naturally remediable of the four missing legs.

Event-asymmetry reading

Two 48-hour catalysts are in the book. EIA crude inventories (Apr-15) carries surprise-dependent asymmetry: post-blockade inventories are highly uncertain, so a significant draw or build moves the tape; consensus risk is genuinely two-sided. TSM Q1 earnings (Apr-16) carries confirmation-dependent asymmetry: March revenue already printed +45.2% y/y, the bar is elevated, and a confirming print is already in the price. The methodology prefers surprise-dependent events — they offer positive convexity — which is why EIA can lift Copper or the oil complex into actionable territory on print, while TSM's upside is largely priced even if the print is strong.

BTC: tactical flip vs data gap

BTC's tactical leg flipped from −1 (in the prior brief) to +1 after the $74.4k reclaim and −7.9k BTC exchange outflow (bullish flow, Grade A). Hash rate is ATH-adjacent, also supportive. However the structural leg (active addresses, Grade A) and the risk overlay (funding, Grade B crowding filter) are both MISSING, so no scored entry is possible. The watchlist short thesis from Memory §5 is increasingly contradicted by the tape and is flagged for removal once the MISSING funding print resolves the crowding question.

Gold/Silver: the crowding trap

Structural + tactical + catalyst all point long, but R−1 on record-high positioning makes entering tonight a chase. Historical pattern for reflation/PGM moves: the worst 90-day drawdowns cluster around exactly these crowded configurations, where one positioning fade cascades into a 5–8% mean-revert before resuming. The professional-regime move is to wait for a skew softening or CFTC spec-long fade, not to pay the top of the range.
