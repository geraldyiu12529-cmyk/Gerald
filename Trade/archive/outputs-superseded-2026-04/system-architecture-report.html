01System Overview

A discretionary cross-asset trading framework enhanced by systematic evidence grading, automated data pipelines, and AI-assisted analysis.

This system was designed to solve a fundamental problem in retail and semi-institutional trading: the gap between academic research quality and real-world trading execution. Most retail traders rely on narrative, gut feel, or isolated technical indicators. Most institutional systems rely on black-box quantitative models with no human judgment layer. This framework occupies the middle ground — it imposes institutional-grade evidence discipline on a discretionary trading process, using an AI-powered data pipeline to ensure every decision is grounded in the strongest available research.

The system trades a fixed universe spanning five asset classes: crypto (BTC, ETH, named altcoins), US mega-cap equities (12 single stocks), ETFs/indices (SPY, QQQ, EWJ, EWY), commodities/metals (Brent, WTI, Gold, Silver, Copper, Palladium, Platinum), and implicitly FX through DXY and cross-currency exposure. Every variable cited in analysis must carry an evidence grade (A, B, or C), with Grade A reserved for findings that are replicated, mechanism-grounded, and economically coherent.

02Theoretical Foundations

Academic underpinnings that drive the scoring framework and variable selection.

Evidence Grading — The Core Innovation

The system's defining characteristic is that no variable enters the scoring framework without a published evidence basis. The three-tier grading system is modeled on medical research hierarchies (systematic reviews > RCTs > observational studies), adapted for financial markets. Grade A variables must be replicated across multiple samples, regions, and time periods with a coherent economic mechanism explaining why they predict returns. Grade B variables work in most samples but may be regime-dependent or have thinner coverage. Grade C variables (small samples, proxy-sensitive, narrative-heavy) are excluded from scoring unless explicitly requested.

This directly implements the McLean & Pontiff (2016) finding that documented anomalies decay 26% out-of-sample and 58% post-publication. By requiring replication and mechanism-grounding, the system filters for variables most likely to survive this decay. The three audit-addition variables (residual momentum, intermediary capital ratio, basis-momentum) were added only after passing this filter, and each carries a built-in 30–50% Sharpe haircut to account for expected decay.

Multi-Factor Composite Scoring

The theoretical basis for the composite scoring approach draws from MSCI's Adaptive Multi-Factor Allocation research, which demonstrated 2.73% active return over MSCI World from 1986–2018. Equal-weight factor combinations are surprisingly competitive due to robustness against estimation error (DeMiguel, Garlappi & Uppal, 2009). The system uses four score components (Structural, Tactical, Catalyst, Risk) rather than the typical two (value + momentum), capturing information that single-factor approaches miss. Research shows 4–6 signal categories maximize information content; beyond 6–8, diminishing returns set in.

Time-series momentum across 58 futures markets. Diversified Sharpe ~1.0. The foundational paper for the system's T-score component.

Validated trend following across 137 years and 67 markets. Positive Sharpe in every decade and asset class. Establishes the persistence of momentum as a real phenomenon.

Volatility-scaled momentum approximately doubles the Sharpe ratio by reducing crash exposure. Directly informs the system's risk overlay and vol-scaling approach.

Residual momentum: 12-month Fama-French 5-factor residualized returns. Post-decay Sharpe 0.6–0.9. Separates stock-specific alpha from factor exposure.

How the components fit together: AI pipeline, data infrastructure, and decision flow.

Component Architecture

The system operates as a scheduled AI pipeline with five daily tasks and two weekly tasks, orchestrated via Claude's scheduled-task infrastructure. Each task produces a markdown output (the human-readable presentation layer) and syncs structured data to an Excel workbook (the analytical layer). Excel is the sole structured data store, holding 9 sheets that track everything from daily variable readings to the signal ledger to audit-addition evidence.

Pre-Flight + Audit Data 19:45

Tests source connectivity, checks cache health, then runs the 4-tier retrieval chain for the three audit-addition variables (residual momentum, intermediary capital, basis-momentum). Writes pipeline-health.json and audit-data staging file.

Market Brief 20:00

Pulls all Grade A variables, scores the macro regime, builds the full asset scorecard with S|T|C|R|Sum columns. Syncs to DailyVariables, RegimeHistory, DataQuality, and CatalystLog sheets.

News & Events 20:10

Searches 7+ categories: geopolitics, macro releases, earnings, crypto regulatory, flash events. Outputs structured file consumed by the trade recommendation.

Trade Recommendation 20:25

Synthesizes brief + news + regime through the 8-step methodology. Outputs decision document, HTML report, and SignalLedger entries. Every signal logged for out-of-sample tracking.

Weekly Regime + Signal Review Sun 18:00

Marks-to-market all open signals, computes hit rates by asset class and score component, identifies methodology improvements across 13 analytical dimensions.

Quarterly Methodology Review Q1/Q2/Q3/Q4

Meta-review of the signal review system itself. Audits analytical dimensions, reconciles research cores against ledger evidence, manages variable candidate pipeline.

04The 8-Step Methodology

A strict sequential process — no step can be skipped, and "no trade" is always a valid output.

1

Regime Identification

Score growth, inflation, policy, financial conditions, and risk-on/risk-off state. For crypto: BTC realized-vol regime, funding/basis crowding, ETF flow direction. Output: one-line regime label + three primary regime variables being watched.

2

Structural Anchor (S-score)

Score slow-moving fundamentals: valuation, quality, revisions, inventories, curve slope, basis-momentum, MVRV regime. Score: +1 supportive / 0 mixed / -1 hostile. Basis-momentum integration: if static slope and basis-momentum diverge, cap S at 0.

3

Tactical Confirmation (T-score)

Score trend (1m/3m/12m), revisions/surprise flow, order flow/positioning. For single stocks, use residual momentum (FF5-residualized) instead of raw TSMOM. Apply Daniel-Moskowitz volatility scaling.

4

Catalyst Map (C-score)

Name the next 1–3 catalysts likely to reprice the asset. State whether thesis depends on surprise vs. confirmation. Score: +1 / 0 / -1. This was identified as a gap in early runs where the C column was omitted entirely.

5

Risk Overlay (R-score)

Score vol state, skew, crowding, liquidity, and intermediary capital capacity. The intermediary capital z-score is a leading indicator — it tightens R before HY OAS widens. Double-counting gate: take the more negative of HY OAS and intermediary capital, not their sum.

6

Score Aggregation

Sum = S + T + C + R. +3 to +5 = Buy/Long. +1 to +2 = Hold/Wait. 0 to -2 = Sell/Reduce. -3 to -5 = Short. Correlation gate: co-moving theses sized to combined sector cap.

7

Expression Choice

Outright long/short when structural AND tactical align. Relative value when ranking is clear but regime is mixed. No trade when signals conflict or thesis depends on single event with poor asymmetry.

8

Invalidation Criteria

Explicit, written, date-bounded. If no concrete invalidation exists, the thesis is narrative, not research-backed — and that means no trade. Every position must have a time-to-invalidation.

05Data Infrastructure & Retrieval

The 4-tier fallback framework that solved the system's single biggest operational risk.

The Problem: Single Points of Failure

The original system had a critical vulnerability: every Grade A variable mapped to exactly one retrieval method. If Yahoo Finance was down, 8–12 variables went MISSING simultaneously, blocking score legs across most of the book. FRED API calls were discovered to be blocked from the sandbox environment entirely. A single failed web search could cascade into a "no trade" output driven by data failure, not market evidence.

Before the fallback framework: A single source outage could produce 8–12 MISSING variables, blocking 5–8 score legs. Two sources down meant 15–20 MISSING, rendering most of the book unscorable.

The Solution: 4-Tier Retrieval Chain

T1

Direct HTTP

Deterministic API calls to Yahoo Finance, CoinGecko, Blockchain.info, Kenneth French library, NY Fed. Timeout: 15s. Fastest and most reliable for available endpoints.

T2

Web Search

2–3 pre-written query formulations per variable, tried in order. Handles URL changes and source migrations. Captures data from news sites, financial portals, and data aggregators.

T3

Persistent Cache

Last known good value stored in /mnt/Trade/.data-cache/. Each variable has a staleness window (1 day for prices, 7 days for weekly data, 60 days for monthly factors). Cache writes happen on every successful T1 or T2 retrieval.

T4

MISSING (Fail-Loud)

Only after all 3 tiers fail. Print MISSING with all attempted sources listed. Leave score leg blank. Log to DataQuality sheet. This is the same fail-loud behavior as before — but now it almost never fires.

Validation Layer

Every retrieved value passes a two-layer validation: static range checks (e.g., VIX must be 8–90) and cache-proximity checks (flags values that deviate more than a variable-specific maximum daily move from the cached value). This catches stale data served as fresh, format changes, and unit mismatches. Ranges widen dynamically if the cached value is near a boundary, preventing rejection of valid extreme moves.

06Risk Management Framework

The binding rules that override all other analysis — every trade must pass the pre-entry checklist.

Position Sizing

Inverse-ATR volatility targeting with quarter-Kelly overlay. Max 25% single-position cap. Full Kelly produces 40–60%+ drawdowns; quarter-Kelly captures ~75% of growth with dramatically reduced variance.

Risk per Trade

0.5–2% of portfolio per position. 0.5% for illiquid names (PLTR, palladium, platinum). Max portfolio heat: 6–8% (sum of all live position risks). Sector cap: 25%.

ATR-Based Stops

Trailing at 2–3x ATR, never fixed-percentage. Evidence: reduces momentum strategy max DD from -49.8% to -11.4% (Han et al. 2016). TP1 at 1.5x ATR, TP2 at 3x ATR.

Circuit Breakers

At -15% portfolio DD: reduce gross to 50%. At -20%: defensive mode (gold + cash overweight, no new risk-on). Prevents catastrophic drawdowns from becoming fatal.

Correlation Gate

Before accepting a |sum| ≥ 3 signal: check if primary regime variable is shared with existing positions. BTC/ETH co-fire ~80% of the time — never counted as independent. Combined bets sized to theme cap.

Pre-Entry Checklist

6 binary gates. If ANY answer is no: no trade. Aggregate ≥ 3? Invalidation written? Correlation clean? Risk ≤ 2%? Heat ≤ 8%? ATR stop set? Catalyst aligned?

Remaining Challenges

Data Correctness vs Availability

The 4-tier framework ensures data is available, not that it's right. A source returning stale or erroneous values is only partially caught by the cache-proximity validation check.

Small Sample Size

The signal ledger has fewer than 60 closed signals. Statistical conclusions about hit rates by dimension, regime, or score component are premature until ~100+ signals.

AI Execution Variability

LLM-driven pipeline tasks may produce slightly different interpretations across sessions. Skill instructions are heavily documented, but some judgment variance is inherent.

Win Rate vs Profitability Paradox
The profitability paradox: 65% of traders have win rates above 50%, yet 82% lose money overall. Average winners are +1.2% per trade while average losers are -2.8%. This system addresses this directly through ATR-based sizing and the requirement that every trade has a defined risk-reward structure (TP1 at 1.5x ATR, TP2 at 3x ATR, stop at 2-3x ATR), ensuring positive expected value even at moderate win rates.

11Token Optimization & Streamlining

How the pipeline was engineered to minimize AI compute costs while preserving analytical quality.

The Problem: Context Window Overhead

Every Claude session starts by loading its system prompt, CLAUDE.md instructions, and relevant memory files. This "startup tax" costs approximately 35,000–40,000 tokens per session — before any useful work begins. With 5 daily tasks and 2 weekly tasks, the original pipeline consumed over 200,000 tokens per day on startup overhead alone. At scale, this means roughly 30–40% of the total token budget was spent reading instructions, not doing analysis.

Daily Token Budget — Before vs After Optimization
Token Savings by Optimization Type

Optimizations Applied

Task Consolidation Biggest Impact

Pre-flight + Audit-data merged into one task: saves one full session startup (35–40K tokens/day). Weekly regime + signal review merged: saves one startup per week (35–40K tokens/week). Eliminates jitter-based ordering risk and guarantees dependency ordering.

Read Overrides High Impact

The merged preflight-audit task skips Methodology Prompt, Memory.md, Risk Rules, Trad core, and Coin core — none needed for data retrieval. The news skill drops Methodology Prompt (it doesn't score or grade variables). Saves 15–25K tokens per task run.

Delta-Check Gate Moderate

Trade-rec Step 0 checks if upstream files have changed since last rec. If nothing changed, exit immediately — no full 8-step rerun. On ~20% of days (weekends, holidays, quiet markets with late-running upstream tasks), this eliminates an entire 80–100K token session.

Conditional HTML Rendering Moderate

Flat days (|Sum| ≤ 1 for all assets) get a compact HTML report instead of full Chart.js visualization suite. Saves 10–15K output tokens on ~40% of trading days. Audit-addition panel always kept for data visibility.

Catalyst-Filtered Searches Moderate

News skill searches only tickers with 48h catalysts individually; rest covered by 1–2 catch-all queries. Typical day: 3–4 targeted searches instead of 12–20. Saves 8–12 web-search calls and their token-heavy results.

Memory-Lessons Redirect Efficiency

Lessons log moved from Memory.md §8 (loaded in every session) to standalone file (loaded only by skills that need it). Prevents unbounded growth of a universally-read document.

Based on the theoretical foundations and the evidence-graded approach, the system should deliver sustained edge above passive benchmarks by combining several compounding advantages: (1) multi-factor composite scoring that diversifies across independent alpha sources, (2) regime awareness that prevents strategy-regime mismatch, (3) structural risk management that limits drawdowns to survivable levels, and (4) a continuous improvement loop through the variable discovery pipeline and quarterly methodology reviews.
