5. Forecast: Challenges & Risks

Signal starvation is the #1 operational risk

In two days, you've produced 0 promoted signals and 9 near-misses. The |Sum| ≥ 3 threshold combined with fail-loud on multiple Grade A variables creates a very high bar. If the system runs for 3-4 weeks with zero trades, that's a design feature (conservative is good) — but it also means zero P&L learning and potential frustration. Watch for: if the promoted signal count is still zero by May 1, consider whether the R-score and fail-loud rules are too restrictive in aggregate, even if each is individually sound.

Web-search data reliability is a structural weakness

Your entire pipeline depends on web searches returning accurate, timely numbers. The residual momentum computation used "approximate" returns from web search, producing near-zero values for all 12 stocks — not because momentum doesn't exist, but because the data quality is low. BTC 3-month basis is persistently MISSING. ACM term premium hasn't updated since January. As the system scales, data gaps will compound. Watch for: if >20% of daily Grade A variables are MISSING on average, the scoring framework is operating on incomplete information often enough to degrade signal quality.

No execution layer means no slippage learning

The hypothetical ledger records entry prices at rec time (20:20 UTC+8 = 08:20 ET pre-open). But real entries face pre-market spreads, gap opens, and order-book depth issues. Commodity entries at stated prices assume futures execution that may not match your actual broker capabilities. Without broker integration, you can't measure the gap between hypothetical and actual fills — and that gap is often 0.5-2% in volatile markets, which erodes edge quickly.

The quarterly review cycle may be too slow for early-stage learning

The next quarterly review is July 1. If a structural flaw in the scoring system exists — say, the C-score adds no information — you'll have 2.5 months of signals before the review catches it. The weekly signal review will flag patterns, but it can't recommend changes (that requires Gerald's sign-off via quarterly review). Consider: should the first few monthly checkpoints be more frequent until the system stabilizes?

Equal component weighting (S=T=C=R) is an assumption, not evidence

Each score leg contributes +1/0/-1 equally. But academic evidence suggests momentum (T) has a higher standalone Sharpe than structural value (S) in most asset classes. If the interaction matrix at 60 signals shows T+1 winning at 70% and C+1 winning at 50%, equal weights are leaving alpha on the table. This is a known trade-off — simplicity vs. optimization — and the right answer depends on how much data you accumulate.
