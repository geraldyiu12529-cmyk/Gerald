Two independent factor sleeves (V029 BAB, V030 DealerGamma), one fundamental quality overlay (V031 GP/A), one issuance anomaly (V032 CEI), and three instances of a trend-following drawdown gate (V033–V035 Faber).

V035 Faber TAA — BTC A Step 1.5 Overlay Gate — crypto Monthly BTC vs 10m-SMA. OFF → crypto sleeve × 0. Shadow OFF
Key design choice: V033–V035 are non-additive to the Sum score. They are a sleeve multiplier applied after scoring. A Sum +4 signal on the crypto sleeve with V035 OFF is logged as a promoted signal with Taken=NO, Block_Reason=OverlayGateOff. This is a drawdown circuit-breaker, not an alpha sizer.

4. Step 1.5 — the Overlay Regime Gate explained

Inserted between the Step 1 regime read and Step 2 scoring. Reads once, flips only at month-end.

How the gate interacts with Sum

Same Sum score, three sleeves. Equity and commodity execute at full size. Crypto sized to zero despite Sum +4 because V035 gate = OFF.

Gate-state transition rules

  • Gate reads the last completed month-end close vs its 10-month SMA
  • Flips only at month-end — no intraday re-evaluation
  • OFF state: sleeve size × 0 regardless of Sum score
  • ON state: sleeve size × 1 (normal scoring applies)
  • Three independent sleeves: SPY (equity), GSG (commodity), BTC (crypto)
  • Drawdown circuit-breaker, not a timing signal
