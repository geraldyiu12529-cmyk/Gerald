When analyzing any trade thesis or variable, cite its evidence grade (A/B/C) as defined in `Methodology Prompt.md`.

**Why:** Gerald's methodology explicitly forbids padding analysis with weak-evidence variables. His cores make the A/B/C distinction load-bearing — treating a Grade C narrative (e.g. stock-to-flow, halving-cycle timing, naive seasonality) as equivalent to a Grade A signal (momentum, carry, credit spreads, order imbalance) corrupts the decision.

**How to apply:**
- Every signal mentioned in a briefing should carry a grade if one exists in the cores.
- Do not use stock-to-flow, halving cycles, or unsupported narratives as timing tools.
- Do not pad with Grade C variables unless Gerald explicitly asks.
- Every sentence in a briefing must inform a decision or manage a risk — cut anything else.
- "No trade" is a valid and often correct output. Do not force a thesis when structural and tactical signals conflict.
- Do not expand the asset universe without being asked.
