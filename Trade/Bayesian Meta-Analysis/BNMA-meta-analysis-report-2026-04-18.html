1Literature Review

1.1 Bayesian network meta-analysis as a methodology

Bayesian network meta-analysis (BNMA) originates in clinical epidemiology, where it was developed to pool direct and indirect treatment comparisons across partially-overlapping trials. The foundational papers are Lu & Ades (2004) on mixed-treatment-comparison likelihoods and Dias, Ades, Welton, Jansen & Sutton (2018, Network Meta-Analysis for Decision-Making, Wiley) on the full hierarchical specification. Application to finance is rare: published work on factor-portfolio meta-analysis is usually frequentist (Harvey, Liu & Zhu 2016; Hou, Xue & Zhang 2020) and single-factor rather than network-structured. The four papers synthesised here adapt the clinical-trials BNMA machinery to trading-variable ranking — with the common-scale likelihood replaced by post-decay Sharpe, the trial as a study-instance, and the asset-class-intercept replacing the clinical sub-population effect.

1.2 The four papers

The four runs are not four independent samples. They are two full-MCMC runs (Paper 1 and Paper 4) and two analytical-approximation runs (Paper 2 and Paper 3). The distinction matters because the analytical approximation silently falls back to the prior when a variable lacks likelihood input, producing posterior_median = 0 with symmetric credible intervals — an artifact that an unprepared reader can easily misinterpret as "4-run evidence of a null effect."

1.3 The primary-source literature for the main variables

The BNMA runs draw likelihood from a heterogeneous academic corpus. A handful of the variables rest on classics; others rest on thin recent work. Understanding which is which informs the verdicts below.

Macro · Rates · Credit

  • V001 VIX — Whaley (2000) introduced the index construct; risk-premium literature is Bollerslev, Tauchen & Zhou (2009) on variance risk premia. Long literature, thin trading-Sharpe evidence in the BNMA.
  • V004 HY OAS / EBP — Gilchrist & Zakrajšek (2012, AER) on the Excess Bond Premium. Hu (2024) supplies an ML-composite extension. Strong lineage; construct heterogeneity between Hu's ML and single-EBP.
  • V005 NFCI — Brave & Butters (2012) Chicago Fed National Financial Conditions Index. Macro-financial diagnostic, not a trading signal — BNMA correctly fails to find a Sharpe for it.
  • V006 UST 2s10s — Estrella & Hardouvelis (1991) on the predictive content of the slope; Rudebusch & Williams (2009) on term structure and recession. Predictive for macro, weak as an entry trigger for equity/crypto.
  • V007 Real yield / breakevens — Beeler & Campbell (2012). Regime-sensitive post-2022 per P3.
  • V008 ACM term premium — Adrian, Crump & Moench (2013, JFE). Information source, not a direct trade.

Cross-Asset · Momentum · Carry

  • V009 TSMOM — Moskowitz, Ooi & Pedersen (2012, JFE); Hurst, Ooi & Pedersen (2017, JPM) for the 100-year extension. The deepest literature of any variable in the framework. Earns its DEPLOY.
  • V010 Revision breadth — Chan, Jegadeesh & Lakonishok (1996). Sharpe-Gil (2024) provides the 6-bin decile evidence. Selection-bias flag on Sharpe-Gil (1 of 6 bins chosen).
  • V011 Brent M1-M3 / commodity curve carry — Erb & Harvey (2006); Gorton & Rouwenhorst (2006). Robust commodity-futures literature.
  • V026 Residual momentum (FF5) — Blitz, Huij & Martens (2011); factor residualisation vs Fama & French (2015) 5-factor model. Positive evidence but subsumed by V009 in every run.
  • V027 Intermediary capital ratio — Adrian, Etula & Muir (2014, JF, "AEM"); He, Kelly & Manela (2017, JFE, "HKM"). Registry currently cites HKM; BNMA evidence says re-anchor to AEM.
  • V028 Basis-momentum — Boons & Prado (2019, JF). Single-paper origin but strong replication; DEPLOY in all four runs.

Crypto / On-chain

  • V012 BTC active addresses, V013 BTC hash rate, V019 MVRV/SOPR — Liu & Tsyvinski (2021, RFS) on crypto cross-section; Griffin & Shams (2020) on manipulation. Mechanism-thin; BNMA correctly rates as INDIST.
  • V014 BTC exchange netflows — Anastasopoulos et al. (2026) on order-flow (cited P1, Sharpe 1.34–1.88); Lee-Wang vol-sort cluster (negative). Two constructs pooled; see Section 9.
  • V015 BTC realized vol, V016 BTC perp funding rate, V018 BTC 3m basis — Koutmos (2018); Alexander et al. (2022). V018 the most promising, but post-ETF regime (2024+) is too short to re-pool.
  • V017 BTC ETF net flows — post-Jan-2024 spot ETF literature. Insufficient sample; EXCLUDE.

Overlay / Regime / Tier-3 candidates

  • C009 Faber TAA — Faber (2007, JWM) "A Quantitative Approach to Tactical Asset Allocation"; 112-year sample and GTAA extension. Only P1 includes it; formal PROMOTE but needs replication.
  • C001/C002/C003 200-DMA, market breadth, VIX term structure — Faber lineage plus Whaley 2000. All WATCH; P4 gives them posteriors near zero with narrow CIs because n=3 peers degenerate.
  • Tier-3 NVT/MVRV oscillators (C001/C002 in P2, C004 in P4) — Yang & Fantazzini (2022, MPRA). Single working paper; DEFER until replication.

2.3 Promotion / graduation criteria (Tier 3 → Grade B)

A Tier-3 candidate is promoted to Grade B only if all three criteria clear in the same BNMA run:

  1. P(μ > 0) > 0.70 — posterior probability that the effect is positive
  2. p_beats_peers > 0.60 — marginal contribution dominance against declared peers
  3. Heterogeneity check pass — τ_med consistent with group-average τ
Result across the 4 runs. Only one variable met all three criteria in any single run: C009 Faber TAA in P1 (p_positive 0.991, p_beats_peers 0.679, τ 0.074). Because it appears only in P1, the meta-analysis graduates it conditionally — registry change admitted, capital commitment pending second-run replication.

3Methodology

3.1 Hierarchical model specification

Each BNMA run fits four parallel hierarchical models — one per Score_Component (S, T, R, Overlay). Within each group, the likelihood pools studies for each variable via a common-scale mean plus an asset-class intercept plus a between-study heterogeneity term:

y_{ij} ~ Normal(μ_j + α_{a(j)}, σ²_{ij} + τ²_g)

where y_{ij} is the reported Sharpe (or sizing-Δ for R-group) of study i on variable j; μ_j is the variable effect; α_a is the asset-class intercept; τ²_g is the group heterogeneity. Priors are grade-tiered (tight on Grade A, looser on Grade B, skeptical on Tier-3).

3.2 Engine choice — full MCMC vs analytical approximation

Full MCMC is the intended engine (PyMC 5.28, non-centred parameterisation, 3–4 chains × 2000 draws × 1500 tune, target_accept = 0.95). Papers 1 and 4 executed this. Papers 2 and 3 fell back to an analytical approximation because the Python compilation environment was missing headers. The analytical fallback returns the prior centre for any variable without likelihood input. This is flagged throughout the meta-analysis.

3.3 Outputs per variable

  • posterior_median, CI95_low, CI95_high — the effect distribution
  • p_positive = P(μ_j > 0) — probability the effect is positive
  • p_top3 — probability the variable is in the top 3 of its group
  • p_beats_peers — probability of dominating declared peers on marginal contribution
  • τ_med — between-study heterogeneity posterior median
  • post_decay_Sharpe — registry-decayed SR for realistic OOS expectation
  • Flags: RANK_UNSTABLE, INDISTINGUISHABLE_FROM_ZERO, HETEROGENEOUS, DIVERGENCE, LOW_POWER_CANDIDATE, GRADE_A_BOTTOM3, TIER3_TOP3, REGISTRY_SHRINKAGE_*σ

3.4 Prior-sensitivity audit

Each paper reruns under a uniformly-loose prior (e.g. N(0, 0.5) for S/T, N(0, 0.25) for R/Overlay). A variable is flagged PRIOR_SENSITIVE if its median rank moves ≥ 3 positions between tight and loose priors. P4 found 6 such variables (V008, V019, C001_P4, C004_P4, V004, V016); P1 found zero; P2/P3 also zero under their analytical scheme.

3.5 Meta-analysis algorithm (this report)

  1. Extract all variable-level posterior data from the 4 papers
  2. Build a cross-run state table keyed by variable ID (V-series) or by name (C-series, because IDs drift across papers)
  3. Compute n_runs_appeared, median_posterior, drift_range, and consensus_p_positive per variable
  4. Apply verdict thresholds (DEPLOY / CONDITIONAL / WATCH / EXCLUDE / DOWNGRADE)
  5. Propose within-group weighting — posterior-proportional, inverse-variance, or equal-weight, by evidence
  6. Test each group against five effectiveness criteria (4×5 grid)
  7. Propose registry actions, trading prescriptions, and next-BNMA priorities

5Discussion — Each Paper

5.1 Paper 1 — BNMA Mark 1 (2026-04-18, full MCMC)

Paper 1 is the strongest diagnostic package of the four. It explicitly documents construct heterogeneity (V001 VIX L/S futures vs vol-managed; V004 EBP Hu ML composite vs single-EBP; V009 TSMOM ML net of TC vs CTA-style), flags registry divergence for V014 (posterior 1.15 vs registry 0.45) with a specific mechanism (Anastasopoulos 2026 order-flow pulling the estimate above Lee-Wang vol-sort), and formally promotes one Overlay variable (C009 Faber TAA). Its audit verdicts — GO on V026, V027 (with AEM re-anchor), and V028 (with headline correction) — are the most operationally concrete of the four runs. The weakness is that P1's posterior for V014 is almost certainly over-reading the one order-flow study's effect; the registry split (order-flow construct vs vol-sort construct) is the correct way to reconcile this rather than accepting the pooled estimate.

5.2 Paper 2 — ChatGPT Mark 1 (undated, analytical approximation)

Paper 2 is informative only for variables that have real likelihood input — V009, V011, V018, V026, V027, V028, plus the two Tier-3 candidates C001 (MVRV/NVRV) and C002 (NVT). For every unlikelihood-input variable, its posterior is literally the prior: median = 0, p_positive = 0.50, symmetric CI. Treating those as real "null effects" is a category error. What Paper 2 does establish is that the analytical approximation, where it moves, agrees with the full-MCMC runs on variable ordering — V028 rank 1 in S, V009 rank 1 in T, V027 rank 1 in R — which is independent corroboration of the spine. Its TIER3_TOP3 flag on V018 (BTC 3m basis) is the clearest flag across the four papers that V018 deserves WATCH rather than EXCLUDE.

5.3 Paper 3 — ChatGPT Mark 2 (undated, consolidated analytical)

Paper 3 is structurally a re-packaging of Paper 2 — same engine, more variable IDs admitted but most marked NO_NUMERIC_INPUT. Its contribution is the prior-sensitivity confirmation (C004 Dual-momentum/GEM moves 1 rank under loose prior — the largest shift found in any of the 4 papers under the analytical approximation, and far below the ≥3-rank threshold) and the explicit tagging of V007 as REGIME_SENSITIVE_POST2022. Both are small but useful incremental findings. Paper 3's value is not its posteriors; it is its methodological annotations.

5.4 Paper 4 — bnma-claude Mark 2 (2026-04-17, full MCMC)

Paper 4 is methodologically the most rigorous. It runs 4 chains × 2000 post-warmup draws, computes posterior-predictive p-values per group (all in 0.05–0.95 band, so no systematic misfit), and does the most comprehensive prior-sensitivity sweep (6 variables move ≥ 3 ranks — all Tier-3 candidates or bottom-ranked Grade A). Its posteriors are materially more shrunk than P1's — V027 posterior 0.24 vs P1's 0.81, V014 posterior 0.03 vs P1's 1.15, V028 posterior 0.39 vs P1's 0.79. This is partly Bayesian shrinkage toward the group mean, and partly because P4 priors are more skeptical (P4 explicitly flags 19 REGISTRY_SHRINKAGE events at 2+σ, versus P1's zero). Neither paper is wrong; they represent different defensible choices on the skeptical-prior vs informative-prior continuum. The meta-analysis uses the P1/P4 centrals as the planning-Sharpe range.

6Discussion — The Meta-Analysis Itself

6.1 What a 4-paper synthesis can and cannot resolve

A 4-paper BNMA synthesis is not a random sample of independent runs. Two of the four (P2, P3) are analytical approximations that corroborate P1/P4 only where likelihood exists. In practice, the effective N is closer to 2.5. That is enough to identify DEPLOY variables — V009, V027, V028 show p_positive ≥ 0.98 in every run where any estimate is produced — but it is not enough to settle construct disputes like V014 BTC exchange netflows, where P1 and P4 disagree by 1.12 in posterior median. For a reader seeking 95%-confidence statements on a contested variable, N ≥ 6 independent full-MCMC runs is the minimum.

6.2 Drift vs noise

The meta-analysis applies a simple rule: posterior drift < 0.15 is noise; 0.15–0.40 is meaningful; > 0.40 is a construct dispute. By this rule, V009 (drift 0.22) and V028 (drift 0.40) are at the edge of meaningful but not contested. V027 (drift 0.57) is meaningful, driven by P1 reporting 0.81 and P4 reporting 0.24 — the registry re-anchor from HKM 2017 to AEM 2014 partially explains this. V014 (drift 1.12) is the only true construct dispute.

6.3 The Bayesian-shrinkage vs informative-prior tension

P1 and P4 differ systematically: P4 is more skeptical (shrinks posteriors harder toward the group mean) and P1 is more informative (lets study-level effects speak). Both are defensible and both converge on the same ranking. The meta-analysis does not take sides — it quotes the P1/P4 range where it matters and uses the central estimate for Stage 4 prescriptions.

6.4 What is missing from the corpus

  • Realised OOS P&L. No paper includes SignalLedger-linked realised performance. The post-decay Sharpes in this report are model extrapolations.
  • Registry change log — we cannot separate "methodology changed" from "evidence evolved" across papers.
  • A common prior-sensitivity protocol — P1 uses Moskowitz-style tight priors; P4 uses grade-tiered priors; P2/P3 use their analytical defaults. Cross-run prior-sensitivity comparison is therefore imperfect.

7.1 Per-group commentary — why the grid reads this way

S — Sentiment / Positioning (3.5 / 5)

The S group works at the top (V028 and V011 discriminate cleanly) and degenerates in the tail (V007/V008/V012/V013/V019 all INDIST in the full-MCMC runs). Scale FAIL is because V011's effect is measured in commodity-curve basis-point convenience yield, while V028 is in time-series-of-curve Sharpe — not the same scale even within the group. This is fixable: restrict S-group study inputs to the long-short-Sharpe scale and accept that commodity-curve carry is a variant of that measurement.

T — Tactical Timing (2.0 / 5)

T fails Discrimination and Asset-class diversity. Of 12 T-variables in P1, 7 are crypto positioning variables that are INDIST from zero in the full-MCMC runs (V014, V017, C006–C011 in P4). The group reads as "momentum + a crypto-positioning waste tail." Actionability is PARTIAL because the surviving tradable variable is V009 alone. Do not regroup; do prune the crypto-positioning tail back to the 2–3 variables that have real mechanism and real study evidence.

R — Regime / Risk (2.0 / 5)

The cleanest failure-by-design. R's explicit purpose is a sizing scalar (a single action), so Actionability PASSING is a feature. But the group is "V027 + 7 variables that don't discriminate" — P4 §8 caveat 5 says this explicitly. The Scale failure is the most important: V001 VIX and V004 EBP are entering BNMA as raw L/S Sharpes ~1.0+, while R's intended effect scale is sizing-improvement Δ ~0.0–0.4. Until study inputs are normalised to the intended scale, R-group posteriors will continue to show the P1 vs P4 gap (V001 posterior 0.72 in P1, −0.03 in P4).

Overlay (1.0 / 5)

Overlay fails four of five because it has three variables. Any group of n=3 is statistically degenerate on rank-width, peer-dominance, and asset-class diversity. Actionability is the only honest pass. The fix is not regrouping but adding variables — promote C009 Faber TAA conditionally (as recommended below), admit 2–3 more regime candidates (MA-ribbon, risk parity, etc.), and let the group reach n≥6 before judging it on discrimination.

7.2 Alternative groupings considered and rejected

  • By asset class (Macro / Equity / Crypto / Commodity). Rejected — makes Scale FAIL worse, because V027 (dimensionless balance-sheet stress) and V004 (bps credit spread) would still sit together as "Macro."
  • By mechanism (Valuation / Momentum / Positioning / Stress). Rejected — creates new peer-dominance failures by pooling V028 and V009 into "Momentum" where V009 would dominate V028.
  • By horizon (Intraday / Days / Weeks / Months). Rejected — orthogonal to the framework's decision logic; many Grade A variables operate on a single horizon by construction.
  • By role (Entry / Sizing / Gate / Exit). Essentially equivalent to current S / T / R / Overlay. A rename, not a restructure.
  • Hybrid 2-axis (Mechanism × Horizon). Rejected — produces more groups, each with fewer variables, worsening the n<6 degeneracy that Overlay already suffers.
Grouping recommendation. Keep S / T / R / Overlay. Enforce R-group effect-scale normalisation (the P1 §8 caveat-1 fix). Prune the T-group crypto-positioning tail. Add 2–3 Overlay variables to break the n=3 degeneracy. Do not restructure the taxonomy.

8Variable Deep-Dive: Why They Work, Why They Don't

Each card below summarises the variable's mechanism, what the 4 runs found, and why the verdict lands where it does. Ordered by verdict.

8.1 DEPLOY — the tradable spine

V009Time-Series Momentum (TSMOM)T · DEPLOY
Mechanism
Trend-following: long assets with positive 12m return, short those with negative. Anchored in Moskowitz-Ooi-Pedersen (2012) with Hurst-Ooi-Pedersen (2017) 100-year replication. Economic story: delayed investor reaction to slow-moving fundamental changes; across-asset momentum is orthogonal to cross-sectional.
4-Run Evidence
p_positive = 1.00 in every run. Posterior median 0.68 (P4) to 0.87 (P2–P3). p_beats_peers 0.66 / 0.99 / 0.99 / 0.95. Only HETEROG flag in P1 (reflects the genuine gap between 0-bp ML studies and net CTA).
Why it works
Deepest academic lineage of any variable in the framework; replicated across asset classes, decades, and methodological specifications. Post-decay Sharpe 0.35–0.50 is consistent with realised CTA-industry Sharpe. The mechanism is robust because it doesn't require a specific market microstructure — only that information diffuses across investors at finite speed.
V027Intermediary Capital RatioR · DEPLOY
Mechanism
Balance-sheet health of primary-dealer banks predicts asset-class-wide risk premia. When dealer capital is stressed, risk premia widen across everything they make markets in. Origin: Adrian-Etula-Muir (2014 JF, "AEM"). Registry currently cites He-Kelly-Manela (2017, "HKM"); BNMA evidence says re-anchor to AEM because AEM's capital-ratio definition (primary-dealer leverage) is the measured quantity, not HKM's broker-sector variant.
4-Run Evidence
p_positive = 1.00 in P1/P2/P3, 0.985 in P4. p_top3 = 0.90 / 1.00 / 1.00 / 0.97. p_beats_peers 0.37 / 1.00 / 1.00 / 0.95 — the P1 weakness is because P1's R group had V004 at rank 1. Three of four runs agree V027 is rank 1 in R.
Why it works
The mechanism is a balance-sheet constraint, not a price pattern. Risk premia are a function of the marginal intermediary's shadow cost of capital — so every risk-asset is linked to this one measure. It functions as a sizing scalar, not an entry, because its signal is the multiplier on gross exposure under stress — halve when z-score below −1σ.
V028Basis-MomentumS · DEPLOY
Mechanism
Cross-asset basis-momentum (Boons & Prado 2019, JF): past changes in the shape of the futures curve predict future returns. Economic story: curve-shape changes reflect supply-demand imbalances that persist — a convenience-yield signal with a momentum overlay.
4-Run Evidence
p_positive = 1.00 (P1/P2/P3), 0.99 (P4). p_beats_peers 0.51 / 0.81 / 0.81 / 0.50 — dominates V011 in 2 of 4. Audit GO in all runs. P1 registry-correction action: headline Sharpe fix from 1.2–1.5 (registry) to 0.9 (paper-level) — the Sharpe claim has been sanity-adjusted.
Why it works
It's a single-paper origin but the internal replication across commodity sub-samples is strong (Boons & Prado test 14 commodities; 11 show the effect). Post-decay Sharpe ~0.39–0.47 is in line. Works because curve-shape changes encode information about storage economics that spot-price changes alone don't.

8.2 DEPLOY_CONDITIONAL

V011Brent M1-M3 Curve SlopeS · CONDITIONAL
Mechanism
Commodity curve carry (Erb-Harvey 2006; Gorton-Rouwenhorst 2006): when the front-month futures price is below the deferred-month (backwardation), investors are paid to hold the future; when above (contango), they pay. The sign of M1 − M3 predicts expected roll return on the curve.
4-Run Evidence
p_positive 1.00 across all 4. p_beats_peers 0.49 / 0.19 / 0.19 / 0.50 — weaker than V028 in 2 runs. Posterior median range 0.40 (P4) to 0.79 (P1).
Why it's conditional
The mechanism is commodity-curve-specific and doesn't cross-apply to equities/crypto/FX. The 2 runs where V028 dominates (P2, P3) are where V011 carries the "BTC-active-addresses universe" penalty from being pooled with unrelated S-group variables. Use it only on commodity curves — don't treat it as a cross-asset sentiment signal.
V026Residual Momentum (FF5)T · CONDITIONAL — sleeve only
Mechanism
Momentum applied to returns residualised against the Fama-French 5-factor model (Blitz-Huij-Martens 2011). Isolates stock-specific momentum by removing market / size / value / profitability / investment factor exposures.
4-Run Evidence
p_positive 1.00 in all 4. But p_beats_peers 0.17 / 0.009 / 0.009 / 0.053 — peer-dominated by V009 in every single run. Posterior 0.39–0.69.
Why it's capped at sleeve deployment
The signal is real but V009 captures the same economic content at the asset-class level without the factor-model imposition. The only portfolios where V026 is worth running are those with a factor-neutrality mandate where V009 cannot be deployed — a narrow equity-long-short sleeve, not the core book. Size at 25% of V009 or smaller.

9.3 Architecture recommendations

  1. Keep S / T / R / Overlay grouping. The 4×5 grid rejects every proposed regrouping. Scale failures are within-group fixes, not taxonomy problems.
  2. Enforce R-group effect-scale normalisation. All R-group study inputs must be on sizing-improvement Δ scale (~0.0–0.4), not raw L/S Sharpe (~1.0+). Recode V001 and V004 inputs before the next BNMA or fail-loud and skip them.
