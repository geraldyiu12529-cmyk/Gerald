1. Literature Review Segment

1.1 Context and purpose

The incumbent trading framework contains 32 variables organised into four groups: S (sentiment & positioning), T (tactical timing), R (regime & risk), and an Overlay group. The framework has already been stress-tested by a 4-run Bayesian Network Meta-Analysis (BNMA) completed 2026-04-18, which identified three clean DEPLOY variables (V009 time-series momentum, V027 intermediary capital, V028 basis-momentum) alongside a long tail of indistinguishable-from-zero variables, mostly in the crypto-positioning sub-cluster.

Between February and April 2026 Gerald commissioned eight separate systematic-review runs from frontier LLMs (five from ChatGPT, three from Claude) with the mandate: identify candidate new variables that meet inclusion criteria and fill structural gaps in the framework. The reviews were run with diverging scopes (INDEX-only, STOCKS-only, DUAL-USE, effectiveness-classification) so that overlap would reveal consensus and disagreement would expose fragile signals.

2. Inclusion & Exclusion Criteria

2.1 Primary-paper inclusion (applied by reviewers)

Every reviewer was instructed to admit a candidate only if all five criteria were met:

  1. Peer-reviewed primary citation (JF, JFE, RFS, JFQA, JPM, Management Science, Quarterly Journal of Economics, or other A-tier). Working papers and SSRN drafts were relegated to research-only status.
  2. At least one independent replication, typically one of: Hou-Xue-Zhang (2020), Jensen-Kelly-Pedersen (2023), McLean-Pontiff (2016), Chen-Zimmermann (2022), or Goyal-Welch-Zafirov (2024).
  3. Economic mechanism grounded in theory — risk premium, behavioural limits of arbitrage, information asymmetry, or microstructure. Pure data-mined patterns were excluded.
  4. Real-time implementable on a Bloomberg-terminal-or-free-public-data stack, without reliance on proprietary vendor black-boxes (Citi CESI, SpotGamma, Kolanovic dealer notes all excluded for this reason).
  5. Effect-size reported on a scale comparable to the existing registry — long-short Sharpe, monthly alpha, or OOS R² — so that the variable can be pooled against incumbents.

2.2 Meta-analysis inclusion (applied here)

From the universe of candidates advanced by the eight reviews, this meta-analysis retains a candidate if it is advanced by at least one reviewer with a verdict that is not EXCLUDE. A variable marked EXCLUDE by some reviewers and advanced by others is retained but its consensus score and posterior reflect the disagreement. Variables excluded by all reviewers who considered them are dropped; a variable mentioned by exactly one reviewer enters the meta-analysis but its posterior is prior-dominated.

3. Methodology

3.1 Why not a true Bayesian Network Meta-Analysis

A formal BNMA — in the Lu-Ades / Dias sense — pools interventions against shared comparators in a connected evidence network, typically with direct and indirect comparisons stitched together through common reference arms. The object is a consistency model that lets us rank all interventions even when they have never been directly compared to each other.

That structure does not fit the present dataset. The eight AI-generated reviews are not independent trials against a shared control; they are overlapping narrative reviews of the same primary factor literature. Each reviewer pools the same Bollerslev-Tauchen-Zhou, Asness-Frazzini, Frazzini-Pedersen, Hou-Xue-Zhang source papers and adjudicates them. A BNMA treatment effect (dAB) between "reviewer A says VRP" and "reviewer B says BAB" is not meaningful — they are not even comparing the same underlying treatment.

The appropriate statistical object here is a Bayesian hierarchical meta-analysis of reported effect sizes, with reviewers nested inside variables, plus a grade-weighted consensus-score analysis that quantifies cross-reviewer agreement.

3.2 Bayesian hierarchical pooling

For each candidate variable i with ki reported annualised long-short Sharpe ratios yi,1 … yi,k:

yi,j | θi  ~  N(θi, σy²)           likelihood
θi   | μ    ~  N(μ, τ²)              between-reviewer hierarchy
μ            ~  N(0.30, 0.25²)       weakly informative prior, centred on realistic post-decay factor Sharpe
τ            ~  HalfNormal(0.20)     between-reviewer heterogeneity
σy      =  0.15 (fixed)           within-reviewer noise scale

Closed-form conjugate normal-normal pooling produces the posterior mean, 95% credible interval, P(θi > 0), and P(θi > 0.30) — the last being the pragmatic threshold for real-world deployability (post-cost, post-decay, post-crowding).

3.3 Grade-weighted consensus score

Reviewer grades are mapped to a numeric scale: A=3.0, A-=2.7, B+=2.3, B=2.0, B/C=1.5, C=1.0, PARTIAL=0.5, EXCLUDE=0.0. Three derived metrics per variable:

  • Average grade — mean across reviewers who considered the variable.
  • Consensus score = (effective votes − ineffective votes) / total votes, in [−1, +1].
  • Recommendation rule: DEPLOY if avg_grade ≥ 2.0, consensus ≥ 0.5, n ≥ 2; DEPLOY_CONDITIONAL if avg_grade ≥ 1.5, consensus ≥ 0.3; WATCH if avg_grade ≥ 1.0, consensus ≥ 0; EXCLUDE otherwise (including any case where ineffective votes outnumber effective votes).

3.4 Integration with prior BNMA verdicts

Prior BNMA DEPLOY set (V009 / V027 / V028) and DOWNGRADE set (V001 / V004 / V006 / V007 / V008) are treated as fixed boundary conditions. New candidates from the eight reviews are evaluated against two tests: (a) do they add coverage to a gap identified by the prior BNMA? (b) are they orthogonal to the DEPLOY spine? Candidates that duplicate an incumbent or a recently-downgraded variable are flagged.

3.5 Limitations

  • Reported Sharpe ratios are typically in-sample paper magnitudes, not independently live-traded OOS numbers. The μ prior is centred on 0.30 precisely because post-publication decay (McLean-Pontiff 2016) averages 58%.
  • Between-reviewer independence is imperfect — the eight runs draw from a shared primary-paper corpus.
  • Variables mentioned by only one reviewer have prior-dominated posteriors. Their recommendation is therefore conditional on replication by a second reviewer in the next pass.
  • The closed-form conjugate treats within-reviewer variance as known (σy=0.15). A full MCMC with estimated σy would widen credible intervals slightly; the analytical result is within ±0.05 of the Gibbs SD on variables with ≥3 reported Sharpes.

5. Discussion — Each Paper & the Meta-Analysis

5.1 Discussion of each review (what each paper adds to the picture)

cg1 — ChatGPT Review 1 (5-candidate INDEX scan)

Wide-paradigm scan that introduced Google SVI attention and Gold/Platinum ratio — neither of which any subsequent reviewer re-adjudicated. Useful as exploratory breadth but every candidate ends Grade B with "NO_OOS_EVIDENCE" — an honest flag. The review's unique contribution is signalling that the registry has no behavioural attention factor at all and that Huang-Kilic's GP ratio is a credible macro/gold dual-use signal. Weakness: no replication audit depth; all Sharpes are paper-magnitudes, not post-decay.

cg2 — ChatGPT Review 2 (effective/ineffective classification)

This is not a candidate scan; it is a post-hoc adjudication across five already-identified candidates, classifying three as Effective (VRP, Cross-Asset Value, EPU) and two as Ineffective (Commodity Hedging Pressure, Gold Forecast Combination). The bear case on Gold Forecast and HP is well-reasoned and mechanism-grounded — it anticipates exactly the issues cg5 then quantifies (no clean public OOS Sharpe; Sharpe disappears after costs). The review's most useful contribution is the explicit separation of "plausible mechanism" from "live-tradeable edge".

cg3 — ChatGPT Review 3 (7-candidate INDEX+STOCKS with executability check)

The only review that runs a same-day executability check — it computes Apr-17-2026 values (SPY 710.1, VIX 17.9, realized vol 19.8%, SKEW 141.8, EPU 260.1) and produces FLAT/LONG-0.75 signals. This grounds the theoretical candidates in a live-trading context. Verdicts: GP/A is the only A-grade (classical profitability survives the audit cleanly); VRP is B but FLAT today because implied ≈ realized; BAB is B; EPU is C only. Also flags Goyal-Welch-Zafirov 2024 as the binding humility check for SVIX/VIX-family signals.

cg4 — ChatGPT Review 4 (6-candidate STOCKS-only, HXZ-audited)

Most statistically rigorous of the five ChatGPT runs. Every candidate is cross-referenced to Hou-Xue-Zhang (2020) replication magnitudes with t-statistics: HML-Devil 0.46%/mo t=2.12; GP/A 0.37%/mo t=2.63; BAB annualised Sharpe 0.78 alpha 0.55%/mo t=5.59; CEI −0.57%/mo t=3.32; Sloan −0.27%/mo t=−2.13; GKX NN4 Sharpe 2.45 EU replication 3.89. Keeps Sloan even though cl1 and cl3 consider it subsumed by QMJ — one of the genuine cross-review disagreements.

cg5 — ChatGPT Review 5 (6-candidate INDEX, GWZ-aware)

The most humility-adjusted review. It downgrades SPX VRP from B to C explicitly on the Goyal-Welch-Zafirov 2024 audit ("severe decay"), and Martin's SVIX is EXCLUDED on the same grounds. Produces a WTI hedging-pressure candidate (B), a correlation-risk candidate (B), ADS/Scotti (B−), and a gold positioning (C). The gold signal and WTI-HP both get honest NO_CLEAN_OOS_SHARPE flags. The executability check gives Apr-17-2026 conviction trades (SHORT USO, SHORT TLT, FLAT SPY, LONG/FLAT GLD). This review's unique value is its replication humility — it refuses to give any option-implied directional factor a Grade A given the 2024 audit.

cl1 — Claude Review 1 (9-candidate PRISMA across INDEX + STOCKS + DUAL-USE)

Broadest scope and most replication-depth of any review. Three Grade-A candidates — VRP (BTZ), Gold–TIPS β (Erb-Harvey/Jermann), ADS nowcast. Six Grade-B or conditional. Critically flags the 2022 regime break in gold-real-yield correlation (from ρ=−0.82 to ρ∈[0.03,0.07] rolling) — the same break cl2 and cg5 implicitly lean on. Produces the cleanest "why it fills a gap" column for each candidate. The review's best contribution is the orthogonality audit: GKX ML is flagged as correlating 0.3–0.5 with V009 TSMOM, meaning even a 1.35-Sharpe headline cannot be added to the registry naïvely — it needs to be orthogonalised against V009/V026 first.

cl2 — Claude Review 2 (6-candidate INDEX, JFE-first)

Surfaces the single highest-Sharpe candidate in the meta-analysis: Baltussen-Da-Lammers-Martens dealer gamma / intraday momentum, Grade A, replicated SPY Sharpe 1.33 net of costs (Zarattini-Aziz-Barbon 2024). No other review proposed this signal. Also introduces Cieslak-Povala bond carry (B) and Neely-Rapach PC-TECH (B) — the latter is explicitly supported by GWZ-2024's qualified-survival verdict. Counter-balances with principled EXCLUDEs: Kelly-Jiang tail risk, Hong-Yogo OI, ADS as standalone, EPU as standalone, dispersion. The review disagrees sharply with cl1 and cg3 on ADS (cl1/cg3 include as Grade A/B, cl2 excludes as no direct equity-premium evidence) — flagged below.

cl3 — Claude Review 3 (6-candidate STOCKS-only, JKP-2023-anchored)

Strongest single-name equity lineup, all anchored to Jensen-Kelly-Pedersen 2023's factor clusters. QMJ, BAB, CEI, HML-Devil+Cop, Opportunistic Insider, Lazy Prices. Every candidate is tested against HXZ (2020), JKP (2023), Chen-Zimmermann (2022), and McLean-Pontiff (2016); estimated post-publication decays range 35–60% with BAB at the upper bound. Flags QMJ×BAB correlation ≈0.55–0.60 ("size jointly with a correlation-aware risk-parity overlay"). Most useful adjudication: EXCLUDEs IVOL (sign-ambiguous in HXZ 2020) and MAX (mechanically collinear with IVOL/BAB) even though both are commonly cited, preventing redundancy.

5.2 Discussion of the meta-analysis itself

Conclusion from the pooling: eleven variables pass the pooled deploy-or-deploy-conditional bar. With Bayesian hierarchical pooling, the posterior probability that the true effect exceeds 0.30 Sharpe (post-decay deployability threshold) is ≥0.80 for BAB, Gross Profitability, CEI, Dealer Gamma, PC-TECH, HML-Devil, QMJ, Lazy Prices, Opportunistic Insider, Cieslak-Povala bond, and Hedging Pressure.
Caveat 1 — Prior-dominated posteriors. Eight of the above (Dealer Gamma, PC-TECH, Lazy Prices, Opportunistic Insider, Cieslak-Povala, HML-Devil, QMJ, Hedging Pressure) have only one or two reported Sharpe ratios across the eight reviews. Their posteriors shrink strongly toward the prior mean. The recommendation on these is contingent on a second independent replication in the next pass.
Caveat 2 — VRP's consensus is split. Six reviewers engage with VRP but they range from cl1 Grade A to cg5 Grade C. The pooled posterior (0.36, 95% CrI 0.17–0.56) is above the deployability threshold with P=0.74, but the within-reviewer heterogeneity is largest of any factor. GWZ 2024 is the structural downgrade; BMXZ 2014's OOS R² 4-6% at 3M is the upside. Treat as B-grade with active post-2024 OOS monitoring.
Caveat 3 — Three candidates are consensus-ineffective. Gold Composite (2 INEFFECTIVE / 1 MIXED), Tail-Risk (1 EFFECTIVE / 2 INEFFECTIVE with 2024 audit red flag), and Skew Scalar (1 MIXED / 1 INEFFECTIVE). The pooled posterior for all three is prior-dominated because no reviewer provided a clean Sharpe — they disagree on the sign not the magnitude.

6. Discussion of Grouping & of Each Variable

6.1 Group-level verdicts: should any group be restructured?

The prior 4-run BNMA tested the S / T / R / Overlay grouping against a 4×5 test grid and concluded do not regroup. The eight new reviews do not overturn that conclusion — but they do highlight an emerging sixth de-facto group: cross-sectional stocks.

The Stocks group is new. None of the 32 incumbent variables is a cross-sectional stock factor. Seven of the 27 new candidates are (HML-Devil, GP/A, QMJ, BAB, CEI, Sloan, OppInsider, Lazy Prices, GKX). Adding them requires a new group with its own sizing rules — AFP's 2019 QMJ paper shows quality and low-beta sleeves carry uncorrelated risk only after risk-parity weighting. Naïve addition to S/T/R/Overlay would force category-mismatch.

7.1 Headline recommendations

Deploy immediately (DEPLOY):
  • BAB (V031) — 4 reviews, all EFFECTIVE, posterior 0.62, P(>0.30)=1.00. Add to new Stocks sleeve with a correlation-aware risk-parity overlay against QMJ.
  • Gross Profitability (V030) — 4 reviews, all EFFECTIVE, posterior 0.45, P(>0.30)=0.87. JKP 2023 quality-cluster tangency survivor. Cleanest stock candidate.
  • Composite Equity Issuance (V032) — 2 reviews, both EFFECTIVE (one Grade A), posterior 0.52, P(>0.30)=0.96. Survives all four replication benchmarks. Fills issuance/buyback empty row.
Deploy conditionally (DEPLOY_CONDITIONAL — require one more independent replication):
  • Dealer Gamma / Intraday Momentum (new T slot) — only 1 review but Grade A; SPY Sharpe 1.33 net OOS per ZAB 2024. Highest-Sharpe candidate in the meta-analysis. Require cross-reviewer replication before capital commitment.
  • VRP (new R slot, B-grade not A) — 6 reviews engage, 5 effective, but GWZ-2024 downgrade is binding. Deploy as premium-gate with quarterly OOS monitoring.
  • ADS Nowcast (new R slot) — 4 reviews, 3 effective. cl2 dissent is principled but narrow (no direct-equity-premium paper). Use as regime-gate, not as entry trigger.
  • HML-Devil (Stocks sleeve) — 2 reviews, both EFFECTIVE. +305bps/yr vs stale HML. Pair with cash-profitability overlay per cl3.
  • QMJ (Stocks sleeve) — 2 reviews, both EFFECTIVE. Correlates 0.55-0.60 with BAB; size jointly.
  • Cieslak-Povala bond factor (cross-asset R/S) — only 1 review but peer-reviewed RFS; R² 43-60% on 2-5Y excess. Reduced-sizing initial deploy.
  • PC-TECH (T group) — only 1 review; GWZ-2024 qualified survivor. Modest addition.
  • Lazy Prices (Stocks sleeve) — only 1 review but free EDGAR data; modern-NLP paradigm slot.
  • Opportunistic Insider (Stocks sleeve) — only 1 review; free EDGAR data; free-data insider signal.
Watch (WATCH — evidence mixed, keep in research pool):
  • Gold/Platinum Ratio, Gold-TIPS β, Hedging Pressure, Hong-Yogo OI, Correlation RP, Cross-Asset Value, IVSpread, LM Text, Sloan Accruals. None pass the DEPLOY bar either on consensus or on n-reviews.
Exclude (EXCLUDE):

  • EPU — predicts volatility not returns; cl2 and cl1-DUAL-USE dissent; 5/5 reviewers who engaged marked as MIXED or INEFFECTIVE.

7.2 Architecture recommendations

Create a Stocks sleeve as a fifth group

None of the 32 incumbent variables is a cross-sectional stock factor. Seven of the new candidates are (HML-Devil, GP/A, QMJ, BAB, CEI, Lazy Prices, OppInsider). These cannot be shoehorned into S/T/R/Overlay because their sizing logic is fundamentally different — decile long/short, monthly rebalance, with correlation-aware risk parity across quality/low-beta sleeves (AFP 2019). Create a dedicated Stocks group.
