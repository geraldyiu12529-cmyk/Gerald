BNMA Statistical Analysis — Computed from 8,000 MCMC Posterior Draws

All comparisons below use joint posterior distributions from the hierarchical Bayesian model (4 chains × 2,000 draws). No ad-hoc comparisons of point estimates.

What is NOT Statistically Possible

Cross-group ranking of R vs S/T: R measures risk-sizing improvement; S/T measure long-short Sharpe. Different units — like comparing meters to kilograms.

Cross-group ranking of Overlay vs anything: Overlay measures conditional Sharpe gain (a modifier). With only n=3 variables, P(top3) = 1.0 trivially — this is arithmetic, not evidence.

Any "Overall Top N" mixing groups: Methodologically invalid. The report's Section 6 "Overall Top 5" was a display convenience, not a statistical finding.

What IS Statistically Valid

Within-group pairwise P(μi > μj) from joint posteriors • Rank distributions • ROPE decisions • S vs T cross-group (same scale) • Heterogeneity (τ) comparisons

8. Methodology Note

Every number on this page was computed from 8,000 MCMC posterior draws (4 chains × 2,000 post-warmup samples) of the hierarchical Bayesian model. No comparisons were made by "just comparing numbers" — all P-values are posterior probabilities from the joint distribution, which properly accounts for uncertainty, correlation between variables, and the hierarchical prior structure.

Where a comparison is not statistically possible (cross-group with different scales, Overlay with n=3), this page says so explicitly rather than presenting a misleading number.
